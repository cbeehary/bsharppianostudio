Welcome

Welcome to B Sharp Piano Studio, the piano studio of Christi Bihary. My goal is to enable every student to achieve his or her own potential at the piano through a balance of fun and challenging factors.

I’ve learned that some of the most critical skills for success in piano lessons are: first, learning how to practice effectively, and secondly, maintaining healthy expectations for both the parent and the student. We will learn how to practice. We will also learn how to produce an excellent sound without becoming discouraged by the pressure of perfectionism. I encourage parents and students to communicate freely with me when they encounter roadblocks or discouragements that they just can’t seem to get through. Together, we can usually find a solution.

1st & 2nd Grade Beginners

Group Lessons

Lessons for beginning students in grades 1-2 take place in a group lesson consisting of 2-4 students. The semester cost is $275. Kids at this age can benefit greatly from being in a group lesson, for these reasons:

  • Kids at this age tend to naturally like to be with other kids, so having a group lesson is fun
  • Being in a group helps with focus and attention during the lesson.
  • It gives motivation to practice during the week, to be prepared for the lesson. (I don't encourage competition, but naturally we all want to do our best among our peers.)
  • There is a benefit in seeing other people excel at some things and struggle with others  - it helps a child to not get so discouraged when practicing something hard - he/she realizes that he/she is not the only one for whom it's hard.

Each group lesson lasts between 35-45 minutes. I teach new concepts to the group, and then each child has his/her turn to play the new songs that he/she practiced at home throughout the previous week. Sometimes I have the kids all line up at the piano at the same time I remove the bench and everyone stands), and we all try a new concept on the piano together.

Private Lessons

3rd Grade & Older

Beginning to intermediate students 3rd grade and older have individual lessons 30 minutes long. The tuition cost for the semester is $300 (books and materials are not covered by tuition). Home practice time will vary based on ability, ranging from 30 minutes to 90 minutes per day. Parents are not required to attend lessons.

Parent Involvement

1st & 2nd Grade

For 1st and 2nd graders, your involvement as a parent is critical. I ask that you attend and observe every lesson, in order for you to be able to guide your child at home during practice times. You will be learning a lot about music and the piano right along with your child, even if you don't have any musical training yourself. I will provide everything you need to help your child.

Your encouragement during home practice times is essential - it will take a lot of hard work on the student’s part for her small fingers to learn to obey what her brain is telling them to do. It will require a lot of patience and wisdom (and sometimes trial and error) from you, as you encourage every small accomplishment, learn when to push your child to try again at something hard, and learn when to back off and relax and just give some things time to develop and not stress her or yourself. I will be there to help you navigate this journey, to help you set expectations and brainstorm new ideas to help your child when she gets stuck.

At the beginning of the semester, you will need to sit with your child throughout almost every practice time at home. Every student should practice 20 minutes every day - if you find you have to practice more than this to accomplish the lesson assignment, then please talk to me and we will figure out what we can do. Towards the halfway point of the semester, your child will be able to do some parts of her assignment on her own as you listen from the next room.

3rd Grade & Older

For 3rd grade and beyond, parents are not required to attend every lesson. However, parents should plan to attend lessons for elementary and middle school children at least once a month to be aware of what we are working on. Parents can also help their child by double-checking at home that the child is working on everything in the lesson assignment.

Regardless of age, encouragement from parents is crucial to every piano student. Please always tell your child whenever you hear a song you like.

FAQs

Do you require us to have a piano at home to practice on?

I strongly recommend getting a piano. Even with the weighted keys that many keyboards now have, pianos and keyboards are still two different instruments. You can find many compact pianos being sold very cheaply on Craigslist, sometimes even free if you are willing to move it. Shop around until you find one you like. You should be able to find a decent starter piano (used) for between $200-$800.

What age do you recommend as a starting age for piano lessons?

I recommend starting piano lessons as a child enters 1st grade. Starting earlier than this presents a unique set of challenges because of the limited attention span and immature muscle control and strength at ages younger than 6 years old. It can be done, but it requires a much bigger commitment from the parent, who will essentially be the “piano teacher” at home during daily practice sessions.

Do you teach music theory, composition, ear training, etc.?

Throughout the year, we will cover a variety of topics. These topics include music theory, music history, composition, ear training, and more. The focus of the beginning of the Fall Semester is on music theory. Towards the end of the Fall Semester, we have fun learning Christmas (and other holiday) music, and we review the concepts learned throughout the semester. In the Winter/Spring Semester, we have monthly themes of music history, February Fun month, ear training, and composition. At the end of the Winter/Spring Semester, there is a Final Exam. The Final Exam has 2 parts: a written portion and performance portion. The purpose of the Final Exam is to draw attention to and reinforce all the concepts that were covered throughout the year.

How does your registration/payment work? Do you schedule week-to-week, yearly, etc.?

My piano program is organized into two semesters: the Fall Semester, which runs from September through December (15 weeks); and the Winter/Spring Semester, which runs January through April (15 weeks). I require every student to register and commit to one semester at a time.

I provide a few payment options, including 1 payment, 2 payments, and monthly payments. More details are given at time of registration.

Are there any student recitals?

Yes, there is a Holiday Recital at the end of the Fall Semester, and there is a Spring Recital at the end of the Winter/Spring Semester.

Do you require recital pieces to be memorized?

No, I do not require memorization of recital pieces, although many students do choose to memorize their recital pieces. Memorization of favorite pieces is something I strongly encourage, and we talk about it throughout the year. Students have different natural abilities to memorize (I knew one brilliant student who could not memorize any song no matter how hard she tried), so I do not have any given requirement for memorizing pieces.

Recitals

Students are required to take part in one recital each semester: the Christmas Recital at the end of the Fall Semester, and the Spring Recital near the end of the Winter/Spring Semester. Students are not required to memorize their recital pieces. However, I do encourage memorization of these pieces, and many students choose to do so.
